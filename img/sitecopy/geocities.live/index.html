What was GeoCities and when did it exist?

GeoCities was one of the first major web hosting services, launched in 1994 as Beverly Hills Internet. It became GeoCities in 1995 and quickly grew into one of the most popular free web hosting platforms of the 1990s. At its peak, GeoCities hosted millions of personal websites organized into themed "neighborhoods" like Area51, Hollywood, SunsetStrip, and more. It was acquired by Yahoo in 1999 for $3.57 billion and remained active until 2009 when Yahoo shut it down.

Why did GeoCities close and what happened to all the websites?

Yahoo closed GeoCities in 2009, citing a shift in focus toward more modern web services. The closure resulted in the deletion of millions of websites, though many were archived by the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine. The shutdown marked the end of an era for personal web publishing, as GeoCities had been home to countless personal pages, fan sites, and early web experiments. Our GeoCities maker helps preserve the aesthetic and spirit of those classic websites.

What were GeoCities neighborhoods and how did they work?

GeoCities organized websites into themed "neighborhoods" that mimicked real-world communities. Popular neighborhoods included Area51 (science fiction and technology), Hollywood (entertainment and celebrities), SunsetStrip (music and bands), WallStreet (business and finance), and many others. Each neighborhood had its own community feel, and users could browse websites within their chosen neighborhood. This unique organization system helped GeoCities become a social platform before social media existed.

What made GeoCities websites so distinctive and memorable?

GeoCities websites were known for their creative, often chaotic design aesthetic. Common features included animated GIF backgrounds, visitor counters, scrolling marquee text, colorful font tags, "Under Construction" banners, guestbooks, web rings, and extensive use of tables for layout. These elements, combined with personal expression and creativity, created a unique web culture that celebrated individuality and DIY web design. GeoCities played a crucial role in democratizing web publishing, making it possible for anyone to create a website without technical knowledge.
